# Bayan — Brand Guidelines

*Last updated: 2026-05-20*

## Brand Purpose
Give Algerian citizens the power to know what's real. A public good — no monetization, no government contract, just clarity.

## Brand Name
**بيان (Bayan)** — means both "official announcement" and "clarity" in Arabic. Quranic resonance: "يا أيها الذين آمنوا إن جاءكم فاسق بنبإ فتثبتوا" (Al-Hujurat:6).

## Taglines
| Context | Tagline |
|---------|---------|
| Primary (on-site) | تحقّق من كل بيان — Verify every announcement |
| Social (viral) | إذا ماكانش في بيان، ماشي رسمي — If it's not on Bayan, it's not official |
| Hero headline | الختم راح. الحقيقة ما تروحش. — The stamp is gone. The truth doesn't leave. |
| Technical | من الختم الأحمر إلى رمز QR — From the red stamp to QR code |

## Brand Archetype
- **Primary: Sage** — wise, truth-seeking, clarity-bringing
- **Secondary: Everyman** — relatable, accessible, for everyone

## Positioning Statement
For Algerian citizens who can't tell if a government announcement is real or fake, Bayan is a free civic tech initiative that lets you verify any announcement in seconds. Unlike checking ministry websites, calling offices, or guessing in Facebook groups, Bayan aggregates all official sources in one place and proposes a QR-based verification standard because the red stamp was designed for paper, not the internet.

## Brand Voice

| Dimension | Do | Don't |
|-----------|-----|-------|
| Authority | Confident, factual, cite sources | Don't lecture, preach, or moralize |
| Accessibility | Use Darija alongside formal Arabic; explain simply | Don't use technical jargon |
| Independence | "We're citizens building for citizens" | Don't sound governmental or political |
| Tone | Calm, clear, helpful | Don't be alarmist or accusatory toward government |
| Humor | Light touch OK in social media | Never sarcastic about the problem itself |

**Personality:** Independent, transparent, civic-minded, technically competent, approachable
**The vibe:** A smart Algerian friend who works in tech and built something useful for everyone.

## Visual Identity Direction (for asset generation)

**Colors:**
- Primary: Deep teal or navy (trust, authority, digital)
- Accent: Warm amber/gold (clarity, light, Algerian warmth)
- Avoid: Red (government stamps), green (Algerian flag — suggests government affiliation)
- Background: Clean white/off-white

**Typography:**
- Arabic: Modern clean Naskh — IBM Plex Arabic, Noto Sans Arabic, or Cairo (NOT calligraphic)
- Latin: Clean geometric sans — Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans
- Verification result must be unmistakable at a glance

**Logo:**
- The word بيان in a clean, modern Arabic typeface
- Optional: subtle checkmark, QR pattern, or clarity metaphor
- Must work at small sizes (favicon, social avatar)
- Must NOT look like a government seal or crest

**Key visual motif:** Before/after — old stamp vs. new QR code

## Brand Story (Hero's Journey)

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Hero | The Algerian citizen trying to verify an announcement |
| Problem | Fake announcements everywhere. The stamp means nothing. Can't trust what you see. |
| Stakes | Wrong decisions, exposed data, eroded trust |
| Guide (Bayan) | A simple tool that gives the answer in seconds. Not the hero — the flashlight. |
| Transformation | From "واش هذا صحيح؟" to certainty. From helpless to empowered. |

## Disclaimer (required on every page)

**Arabic:** هذا مشروع مستقل وليس موقعًا حكوميًا رسميًا. بيان مبادرة تطوعية مفتوحة المصدر.
**French:** Ce site est un projet indépendant et ne constitue pas un site gouvernemental officiel.

## Words to Use
تحقّق (verify), بيان (announcement/clarity), رسمي (official), مزوّر (fake/forged), صحيح (authentic)

## Words to Avoid
Technical jargon (OCR, cryptographic, fuzzy matching), government/bureaucratic language, "AI"/"deepfake" in citizen-facing copy
